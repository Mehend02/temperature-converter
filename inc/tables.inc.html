Conversion Formulas Used
Celsius to Fahrenheit °F = 9/5 (°C) + 32
Celsius to Kelvin K = °C + 273.15
Fahrenheit to Celsius °C = 5/9 (°F - 32)
Fahrenheit to Kelvin K = (°F + 459.67)× 5/9
Kelvin to Fahrenheit °F = (K × 9/5) - 459.67
Kelvin to Celsius °C = K - 273.15
Facts about Temperature
Water boils at 212 degrees Fahrenheit and 100 degrees Celsius.
Water freezes at 32 degrees Fahrenheit and 0 degrees Celsius.
Celsius is the most commonly used temperature measurement in the world. Only four countries, including the United States, do not use it.
Zero Kelvin is the coldest temperature and the lowest energy level.
Unlike Celsius and Fahrenheit, the Kelvin scale does not measure temperature in degrees.
